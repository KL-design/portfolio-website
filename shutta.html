Shutta: an e-commerce site for camera enthusiasts

UI and light UX

Shutta is a premium, vintage camera and accessory shop dedicated to providing quality and genuine equipment for both new and seasoned photography enthusiasts. What gets us up in the morning is the chance to provide someone with that creative outlet, nostalgic collection piece, or fantastically unique gift for someone special. This responsive web app offers registration-free browsing and guest check-out, home delivery, advanced filtering, product ratings and reviews, as well as painless & free returns; but it doesnâ€™t stop there - registered users are able to take advantage of our wish list feature, search and purchase history, exclusive mailing list discounts, and personalized product recommendations. Shutta provides the seamless, sleek, and intuitive online shopping experience every customer hopes for with a clean and modern, yet vintage, feel.

My Process

Ideation

After defining my goal, user needs, and product, I got to work on sketching out a user flow to help me visualize the user's process and plan out the screens that may be needed for them to achieve their goals.

Wireframes

Sketches in hand, I took to Figma to digitize my screen ideas. Once I got going with my digital mid-fidelity wireframes, I ended up adding a couple more screens to help flesh out the flow.

Design Inspiration

With wireframes started, I needed to get clear on my visual design direction and branding for this product. I knew I wanted to go bold with a stark black and white contrast to achieve the clean feeling interface, but I took to Google and Pinterest to gather some inspiration.

Final Designs

With branding complete, I proceeded to bring my mid-fidelity wireframes up to high-fidelity detail, using Figma. I ended up adding a few more screens here as well to include screen states for prototyping, such as empty vs. filled fields.

I married clean, white product imagery with sepia-toned imagery to achieve that modern vintage feel and tie in the accent colours. Thin lines and body text help to make this interface feel modern and minimal.

Conclusion

Aside from choosing the design direction, my favourite aspect of this project was definitely the user testing. I thoroughly enjoy discovering the blind spots and gaining new perspectives that create impact through design solutions!

As this was my first e-commerce project, my biggest challenge was learning and designing for common and current e-commerce design patterns. Despite being an online shopper myself, I was surprised at how few of the patterns I have plenty of experience with I could recall. So hats off to the designers of my favourite online stores - the best UX is invisible!

Going forward, I'd like to conduct some UI preference testing, do additional user testing to challenge or validate design solutions currently implemented for this first iteration, and pull out all the stops to fully flesh out the UX for this project.
